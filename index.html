Covid 19 Pandemic In Malaysia

A Visualization by Johan Azlan (31165001)

The Visualization analyzes the Covid 19 Cases and Vaccinations during June 1st 2021 to September 1st 2021. Malaysia was constantly reporting a surge of daily cases during this period of time. At the same time, the vaccination program was ramping up in order to achieve 80% vaccinated Malaysians by February 2022. Hence, this visualization aims to provide further insights on the cases and vaccinations in Malaysia.

Covid 19 Cases

Cumulative COVID-19 Cases per 1,000 population from 1st June 2021 to 1st September 2021

Malaysia's Covid 19 Cases has been increasing since the start of 2021. The daily covid cases reached its peak on 26 August 2021 with 24,599 daily cases. This is an alarming situation for Malaysia as there has been multiple lockdowns established but the cases continues to rise. The map shows the cumulative Covid 19 cases per 1k population during June 1st 2021 to September 1st 2021 for each state. We can take a look at the states with the most Covid cases during this period.

The line chart above shows the daily cases and daily recoveries from 1st June to 1st 2021. It is important to view the trends of daily cases and daily recoveries. It provides a glimpse into the Covid 19 situation in Malaysia.

The donut chart shows the percentage of Covid 19 cases in different categories. Based on the visualization, the majority of the cases come from workplaces (62%), community areas (27%) and detention centres (6%).

Vaccination

Vaccinations are important as it helps to protect us from the deadly virus. Malaysia has been increasing their vaccinations starting from June 1 2021. This was done to achieve herd immunity as fast as possible. Malaysia aims to reach 80% vaccinated population by February 2022 as it transitions towards endemicity. The 3 visualizations below show the daily vaccination doses administered from 1st June 2021 to 1st September 2021, vaccination progress up to September 2021 and the proportion of vaccine brands used.

The stacked bar chart shows the daily doses administered for partial doses and also full doses. The period with the most daily doses administered is in the month of August 2021. The highest doses administered was on 28 July 2021 with 206,288 fully vaccinated and 373,486 partially vaccinated.

The waffle chart shows the vaccination progress up to 1st September 2021. 47% Malaysians have been fully vaccinated and 61% Malaysians are partially vaccinated . Thus, 61% of the total population has at least 1 dose while 39% of the total population are unvaccinated.

The single horizontal stacked bar chart shows the vaccine brands used from 1st June 2021 to 1st September 2021. The vaccine that was used the most during this period of time was Sinovac (48.9%) followed by Pfizer (41.4%), AstraZeneca (9.6%) and Cansino (0.1%).
